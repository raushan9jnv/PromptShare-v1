Prompt engineering is the practice of crafting inputs to AI models that produce **better, more reliable outputs**. Whether you are using ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any other LLM, the quality of your prompt directly determines the quality of the response.

## Why Prompt Engineering Matters

The difference between a mediocre prompt and a well-crafted one is **dramatic**. Consider these two approaches:

```text
Bad:  "Write me a blog post about AI"
Good: "Write a 500-word blog post for a tech-savvy audience about how
       small businesses can use AI chatbots to improve customer support.
       Include 3 specific examples and a practical getting-started section."
```

The second prompt gives the model **context**, **constraints**, and **structure** - three pillars of effective prompt engineering.

## The Core Framework: RICE

We recommend the **RICE** framework for structuring prompts:

- **R**ole - Tell the AI who it should be
- **I**nstructions - What exactly to do
- **C**ontext - Background information
- **E**xample - Show what good output looks like

### Role

Setting a role primes the model's behavior:

```text
You are a senior copywriter at a Fortune 500 company with 15 years of
experience in B2B SaaS marketing.
```

### Instructions

Be specific about format, length, and tone:

```text
Write 5 LinkedIn post ideas. Each should be under 200 words, use a
conversational tone, and end with a question to drive engagement.
```

### Context

Give the model information it needs:

```text
Our company sells project management software to remote teams. Our
target audience is engineering managers at mid-size startups (50-200
employees). Our brand voice is professional but approachable.
```

### Example

Show, don't just tell:

```text
Here's an example of our preferred style:
"Remote standup meetings don't have to feel like a chore. Here's how
we turned our daily 15-minute sync into the most productive meeting
of the day..."
```

## Temperature and Top-P

Two key parameters affect output randomness:

| Parameter | Low Value | High Value |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **Temperature** (0-2) | Focused, deterministic | Creative, varied |
| **Top-P** (0-1) | Conservative word choices | Diverse vocabulary |

> **Tip:** For factual tasks like code or data extraction, use temperature 0-0.3. For creative tasks like brainstorming or copywriting, try 0.7-1.0.

## Zero-Shot vs Few-Shot

**Zero-shot** means giving instructions without examples:

```text
Classify this review as positive, negative, or neutral:
"The battery life is amazing but the screen could be brighter."
```

**Few-shot** means providing examples first:

```text
Classify reviews as positive, negative, or neutral.

Review: "Love this product!" -> positive
Review: "Worst purchase ever." -> negative
Review: "It's okay, nothing special." -> neutral

Review: "The battery life is amazing but the screen could be brighter." ->
```

Few-shot prompting typically improves accuracy by **15-30%** for classification tasks.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Being too vague** - "Make it better" tells the model nothing
2. **Overloading** - Trying to do 10 things in one prompt
3. **No format specification** - Not telling the model how to respond
4. **Ignoring iteration** - Treating prompting as one-shot instead of a conversation
